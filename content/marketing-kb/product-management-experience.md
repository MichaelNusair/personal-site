## Overview

Before becoming a full-stack engineer, Michael spent a **full year as a Product Manager** at a defense technology organization — and he carried that product mindset into every engineering role since. This isn't an engineer who waits for tickets. This is an engineer who understands *why* features exist, how to prioritize them, and how to deliver them on schedule.

## Formal PM Role (2020–2021) — DefenseTech

### Scope

Michael managed the development and deployment process for new systems and releases within the control division. He represented **hundreds of operational users** while driving product development under strict operational constraints.

### What He Did

**Requirements & Specification**
- Translated complex, real-world operator workflows into clear technical specifications and RFCs
- Defined acceptance criteria that bridged operational reality and engineering feasibility
- Worked directly with end-users in the field to understand pain points and workflows

**Roadmap & Prioritization**
- Partnered with leadership and engineering to prioritize the product roadmap
- Balanced urgent operational needs with long-term architectural improvements
- Made tough trade-off decisions under resource constraints

**Cross-Functional Execution**
- Coordinated delivery across frontend, backend, and infrastructure teams
- Managed risk assessment and deployment rollout strategies for mission-critical releases
- Authored release notes and conducted user training sessions

**Incident Response & Feedback Loops**
- Partnered with on-call and support teams to triage critical production issues
- Led root-cause analysis sessions, ensuring learnings fed back into the product backlog
- Built systematic feedback loops between operations and engineering

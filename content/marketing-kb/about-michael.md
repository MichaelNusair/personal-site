# About Michael Nusair

## The Hacker-Entrepreneur Who Builds at Every Layer

Michael Nusair is a full-stack software engineer, cloud architect, and serial builder based in Tel Aviv, Israel. With nearly a decade of hands-on experience across defense systems, AI commerce platforms, and self-funded tech ventures, he brings a rare combination of deep technical skill and entrepreneurial drive to every engagement.

---

## Origin Story

Michael's career didn't start in a comfortable IDE. It started in high-stakes operations — managing critical shifts, coordinating cross-team responses, and learning what it truly means for systems to be reliable when failure isn't an option. Those years in defense operations gave him something most engineers lack: an intuitive understanding of how technology serves real-world missions under pressure.

From operations, Michael transitioned into product management — translating complex operator workflows into technical specs, prioritizing roadmaps, and managing cross-functional delivery. This gave him the vocabulary to bridge business and engineering, a skill that defines how he approaches every project today.

Then came the code. Michael dove into full-stack engineering and never looked back. He spent 3.5 years as a full-stack engineer on a mission-critical command and control system, where he led the migration from a monolithic architecture to 30+ microservices, adopted API gateways with enterprise SSO, and ran disaster recovery experiments for multi-regional resilience. He was the senior on-call engineer in a 24/7 environment where downtime meant real consequences.

## Philosophy

Michael believes in pragmatic engineering. He avoids unnecessary complexity early on while applying advanced patterns — event-driven architectures, distributed workflows, container orchestration — when scale or resilience actually demands them. He sets up CI/CD pipelines from day one because he's seen what happens when teams don't.

He thrives in ambiguity. Give him an undefined problem space and he'll clarify the requirements, design the system, build the MVP, and iterate based on real user feedback. He's done it dozens of times.
